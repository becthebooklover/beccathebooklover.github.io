ACTUAL REVIEW

This book was so effin amazing you have no idea. There was a love triangle, of course, but it ended in the best possible way. You have no idea how stressful it was reading the epilogue. I have never cried so much about a book, never. It was beautiful and sad and stunning and you want to smile because you kind of get what you want but then the EPILOGUE JUST HITS YOU. It's like shoving a rainbow in your face and then turning the rainbow into a thousand spiders. I won't say anymore, otherwise I will be spoiling you, but it was heart-wrenching, in a good way. There are some Wessa/Jessa make scenes in this book, nothing too mature. The writing is easy to understand, although there are one or two points in this book where you're just like, "oh, okay, we get it, this is 1880 Britain, they speak weirdly, but can you go back to regular talk now?" There is romance in this book, but its not the main focus, and it's very well written, maybe the best I've ever read. This isn't one of the of the books that finish abruptly (case in point: matched, cinder, wtc), thank goodness, although it might be because I was crying most of the time. Are you a big book crier? The crying parts started around the halfway point, and from then, tears just start flowing. And this book is hilarious too, so I looked crazy reading it on the plane. Imagine a person across the aisle with tears rolling down her face, sniffling softly, then suddenly she bursts into cackles- there's snot and tears everywhere - and I didnt have a napkin so I had to go to the plane bathroom to calm myself down. Never read this in public, people. It's way too emotional.
